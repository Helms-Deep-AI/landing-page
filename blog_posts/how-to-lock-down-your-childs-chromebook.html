Why Locking Down the Chromebook Matters

Middle school is when kids' curiosity accelerates. They are also deeply vulnerable to:

  • Mind-viruses (harmful ideologies and toxic communities)
  • Social engineering and manipulation
  • Digital addiction (gaming, endless videos, social media loops)
  • Exposure to inappropriate content
  • Scams and exploitation

The default Chromebook settings do not protect against these risks. Even school accounts can't fully filter YouTube, Google searches, or third-party apps. That's why we recommend a whitelist-only approach: your child can access only the sites and tools you explicitly allow. Everything else is blocked.
