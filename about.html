Who do you want to protect?

Boys face unique challenges in the digital world that can significantly impact their development and future success.

The primary risk for boys is finding fulfillment in digital simulations rather than real life, leading to a decline in achievement and engagement with school, work, and family. Boys are increasingly at risk of "failing to launch" and becoming NEETs (Not in Education, Employment, or Training).

The digital threats targeting your sons include:

  • Video Games: 7% of boys develop serious addictions to video games, leading to declining physical and mental health, family strife, and failure to launch. These games provide artificial achievement that feels real but builds no actual skills.
  • Pornography: Early exposure to inappropriate content can harm emotional development and promote unhealthy relationships and expectations.
  • Female Streamers: Risk of developing parasocial relationships or exposure to suggestive content that distorts views of real relationships.
  • AI Girlfriends: Encourages isolation, emotional dependency on virtual entities, and can hinder real-life social skill development.
  • Power Fantasies: Overexposure to power-driven narratives that may falsely fulfilled desires for accomplishment. Boys end up shunning real-world risk, which is necessary for development into healthy men.
  • Social Media: Provides a fake "wolf pack" for boys, with none of the long-term trust or real-world support. Increases anomie due to lack of stable connections, breeding despair, suicide, and a sense of meaninglessness.

Helm's Deep helps protect your sons by creating a digital environment that encourages real achievement, healthy relationships, and meaningful connections. We help you guide your sons toward productive digital experiences while blocking harmful content that could derail their development.

Girls face distinct challenges online that can significantly impact their mental health, self-image, and social development.

Research shows that girls are more harmed by social media than boys, with higher rates of depression and other disorders correlated to usage. The digital world presents unique risks to your daughter's wellbeing and future.

The digital threats targeting your daughters include:

  • Social Media: Girls use visual platforms like Instagram and TikTok more than boys, worsening comparison culture. "Beauty Wars" occur where girls feel pressure to adopt tools like filters, makeup, and surgery to compete. Social media also offers ways for girls to attack rivals by damaging relationships and reputations.
  • Online Shopping: Risk of impulsive spending or being influenced by consumer-driven trends and marketing tactics that exploit insecurities.
  • Romance Novels/Content: Unrealistic portrayals of relationships and unhealthy emotional expectations that can distort views of healthy partnerships.
  • Predators/Social Engineers: Girls are more likely to be groomed by online predators posing as trustworthy individuals. The effort and risk for men to stalk or approach girls online is low, with minimal accountability.
  • Sociogenic Illnesses: Girls are more vulnerable to "sociogenic" illnesses, or social contagion, where illnesses are psychosomatic rather than biological. Online filter bubbles can exacerbate this issue.

Helm's Deep helps protect your daughters by creating a digital environment that promotes healthy self-image, authentic relationships, and meaningful content. We help you guide your daughters toward enriching digital experiences while blocking harmful content that exploits vulnerabilities.
